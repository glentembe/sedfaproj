Welcome to Sedfa

On 1 October 2024, agencies – Small Enterprise Finance Agency (sefa) and Small Enterprise Development Agency (Seda) under the Department of Small Business Development, and the Cooperative Banking Development Agency (CBDA) from National Treasury – officially came together to form the Small Enterprise Development and Finance Agency, better known as Sedfa.

More About Us

Sedfa is established in terms of the National Small Enterprise Amendment Act 21 of 2024 (NSEAA), which amended certain provisions of the National Small Enterprises Act 102 of 1996 (as amended), the Co-operatives Act 14 of 2005 (as amended), and the Co-operative Banks Act 40 of 2007 (as amended). In terms of NSEAA and the Companies Act 71 of 2008 (as amended), the SEDFA Memorandum of Incorporation is lodged as a state-owned company (SOC) with limited liability (SOC Limited), and Sedfa conducts its business as a corporate entity with the state as its sole shareholder, represented by the Minister of Small Business Development as Executive Authority, and acts through its board.

The Sedfa has been established as a single integrated institution to:

  • Expand the reach of government enterprise development support to address a greater number of small enterprises and cooperatives across the country. 
  • Ensure that small enterprise support interventions are accessible and make maximum impact.
  • Pull limited resources to create a larger, more viable base for investments. 
  • Transform the way government works and engages with the broader ecosystem and rebuild trust and effective partnerships. 
  • Rationalise limited resources to ensure that more money is available to small enterprises. 
  • Establish an entity that will be agile enough to identify and take advantage of opportunities to grow its resources to reinvest in small enterprises sustainably. 

Sedfa provides comprehensive, integrated financial and non-financial support services to build and sustain competitive small enterprises and cooperatives. Sedfa offers customised services that guide small enterprises at every stage of their growth journey.

Our Vision

Empowering South Africa’s small businesses for inclusive growth, transformation and sustainable employment

Our Mission

To provide targeted financial and non-financial support to Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises, and Co-operatives, as well as to promote and coordinate South Africa’s small enterprise development ecosystem

Our Values

Sedfa subscribes to the following organisational values, I-CARE, which are in line with the Batho Pele principles:

I: Integrity

  • We uphold integrity through transparent practices, open and honest communication, and ethical decision-making. 
  • We strive at all times to build trust and credibility within the agency and with our ecosystem partners.

C: Customer-centric excellence

  • We place the customer at the centre of all our actions by listening, understanding and addressing the evolving needs of small enterprises.. 
  • We strive for excellence by creating an enabling environment and consistently providing tailored support and solutions at all entry points of the business growth path.

A: Agility

  • We create a supportive and nurturing environment that encourages persistence, agility, and adaptability among entrepreneurs, empowering them to navigate challenges, seize opportunities, and thrive in a dynamic business landscape.
  • We act with speed and urgency in delivering support to small enterprises, ensuring that our efforts contribute to the NDP’s goal of generating the majority of new jobs through small enterprises.

R: Resilience

  • We seek to proactively recover from setbacks, and to marshal available resources, ask for help when needed, and find ways to manage challenging situations as they arise.
  • We use our integrated systems to assess our environment and to identify and assess risks and vulnerabilities, and then implement strategies to mitigate, transfer, or manage risks.

E: Entrepreneurial passion

  • We are solutions-driven and demonstrate unwavering passion for fostering entrepreneurship, igniting the spark of innovation, and driving meaningful change within the enterprise development ecosystem.
  • We create an enabling environment for co-operative banking institutions to deliver community-driven financial solutions that empower members to collectively build prosperous and sustainable local economies.

Our Services

Business Development

We help small businesses with basic support, including formalisation, marketing and business plans to start and grow small enterprises and cooperatives. Sedfa also provides support to small to medium enterprises to be sustainable and competitive through:

  • Sedfa Enterprise Coaching Programme – Personalised guidance to build your business skills.
  • Empretec – A globally recognised training programme for entrepreneurial development.
  • Manufacturing Supplier Programme – Support for small manufacturers to meet supplier standards.
  • Export Development & Trade Facilitation Programme – Helping businesses expand into international markets.
  • Supplier Development Programme – Strengthening your ability to supply larger companies.
  • Township and Rural Support – Tailored assistance for businesses in townships and rural areas.
  • Technology Transfer Assistance Programme – Increases accessibility to technologies and management support for SMMEs.
  • Entrepreneurship in Schools – Focuses on introducing entrepreneurship in schools.
  • International Trade Centre (ITC) SheTrades Programme – Upskills South African women entrepreneurs technical and business skills.
  • Pitch for Funding Programme - Bridging the gap between entrepreneurs' needs and investors' expectations.
  • Incubation Programme - Access to infrastructure, technical, and business development support.
  • Other Business Development Services :

    • Franchise awareness
    • Business start up training
    • Business planning
    • Business Registration
    • Business start up advice
    • Entrepreneurial training courses

    Small and Medium Enterprises

    • Access to export opportunities

    • Access to technology
    • Quality Management Standards Development, Product Testing and Certification
    • Facilitation of access to finance
    • Business mentoring

    People with Disabilities

Financial Support

We offer a range of funding options to help small businesses access the money they need to grow:

  • Asset Finance & Term Loans – For buying equipment or growing your business.
  • Bridging Loans & Revolving Credit – For managing cash flow and short-term financial gaps.
  • Sector-Specific Funds – Special funds for key areas like:
    • Township and Rural Entrepreneurship (TREP)
    • Youth Challenge Fund (YCF)
    • Amavulandlela Scheme
    • Small Enterprise Manufacturing Support
  • Wholesale Lending – Partnering with intermediaries to reach more businesses.

Cooperative Banking Promotion & Advancement Support

We support Cooperative Banking Institutions (CBIs) and Cooperative Financial Institutions (CFIs) by helping them grow and improve through:

  • Cooperative Banking Promotion & Advancement
    • Cooperative banking model, promotion and awareness campaigns through webinars, events and media
    • Pre-registration support
  • Training & Technical Support
    • Operationalisation
    • Training
    • Custom technical assistance
    • Mentorship & Coaching
  • Financial Support
    • Access to Cooperative Development Support Programme
    • Wholesale finance for on-lending, through SEDFA available funding mechanisms and other external
    • Liquidity Support
    • Stabilisation fund for struggling CBIs
  • Cooperative Banking Advancement Services
    • Digitisation
    • Access to National Payment System
    • Support of National Secondary Coop Bank
    • Support of Cooperative Banking Sector Association
    • Research
  • Business Development
    • Social facilitation and mobilisation
    • Cooperatives development and registration

Client Support

Pre Investment

Sedfa's Non Financial Support

  • Client visits a Sedfa branch
  • Practitioner consults with a client
  • Practitioner registers client on the Sedfa Portal
  • Client signs Confidentiality Service Agreement
  • Practitioner conducts a business assessment
  • Practitioner develops business case
  • An intervention is agreed upon and implemented
  • Process is signed off upon completion
  • Practitioner facilitates service provider payment
  • Practitioner conducts a customer satisfaction survey and identifies areas for improvement
  • Practitioner documents a success story
  • Practitioner conducts an Improvement Assessment in the sixth month after sign-off.

Investment Support

Sedfa's Financial Support

  • Client access the Sedfa website
  • Navigate to and click “Apply for Funding”
  • Client selects the product they wish to apply for
  • Client captures the application information, attaches support documentation and submits their application
  • Application basic assessment is conducted (application completeness)
  • Aplication due dilligence is conducted (Credit, Risk and Legal)
  • Funding Approval is conducted
  • Contracting is conducted
  • Funding Disbursement is conducted

Post Investment

Sedfa's Post Financial Support

  • Monitor Business Performance Reporting
  • Provide Business Support services
