Welcome to Sedfa

Empowering Small Enterprises for Inclusive Growth & Sustainable Development

As of 01 October 2024, the Small Enterprise Development and Finance Agency, Sedfa commenced its operations. This new entity was formed when the Small Enterprise Finance Agency (sefa), the Small Enterprise Development Agency (Seda), and the Cooperative Banks Development Agency (CBDA) officially merged to create (Sedfa) under the National Small Enterprise Amendment Act of 2024 (NSEA).

Sedfa (Small Enterprise Development & Finance Agency) is committed to promoting and supporting small enterprises across South Africa. We provide financial and non-financial support to help businesses grow, transform, and contribute to sustainable employment.

Our Vision

Empowering South Africa’s small enterprises for inclusive growth, transformation and sustainable employment

Our Mission

To promote and coordinate South Africa’s small enterprise development ecosystem, deliver targeted financial and non-financial support, develop co-operative banking institutions, and strengthen the capacity of intermediaries to provide quality support to small enterprises

  • Export Development Programme

  • Township and Rural Support

Financial Support

We offer various financing solutions to help small businesses access capital, including:

  • Asset Finance & Term Loans – Supporting business expansion and working capital.
  • Bridging Loans & Revolving Credit – Helping businesses manage short-term financial needs.
  • Sector-Specific Funds – Including the Township & Rural Entrepreneurship Programme (TREP), Youth Challenge Fund (YCF), and Tourism Equity Fund, Amavulandlela Funding Scheme and Small Enterprise Manufacturing Support.
  • Wholesale Lending – Providing structured finance solutions through intermediaries.

Cooperative Development

We strengthen and develop Cooperative Banking Institutions (CBIs) through:

  • Capacity building & technical assistance
  • Strategic funding partnerships
  • Automation & digital banking solutions

Client Support

Pre Investment

Sedfa's Non Financial Support

  • Client visits a Sedfa branch
  • Practitioner consults with a client
  • Practitioner registers client on the Sedfa Portal
  • Client signs Confidentiality Service Agreement
  • Practitioner conducts a business assessment
  • Practitioner develops business case
  • An intervention is agreed upon and implemented
  • Process is signed off upon completion
  • Practitioner facilitates service provider payment
  • Practitioner conducts a customer satisfaction survey and identifies areas for improvement
  • Practitioner documents a success story

Investment Support

Sedfa's Financial Support

  • Client access the Sedfa website
  • Navigate to and click “Apply for Funding”
  • Client selects the product they wish to apply for
  • Client captures the application information, attaches support documentation and submits their application
  • Application basic assessment is conducted (application completeness)
  • Aplication due dilligence is conducted (Credit, Risk and Legal)
  • Funding Approval is conducted
  • Contracting is conducted
  • Funding Disbursement is conducted

Post Investment

Sedfa's Post Financial Support

  • Monitor Business Performance Reporting
  • Provide Business Support services
