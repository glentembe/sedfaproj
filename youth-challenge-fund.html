Youth Challenge Fund

Overview

The Youth Challenge Fund (YCF) is a funding initiative by the Minister of the Small Business Development in partnership with the EU EDSE Support Programme, implemented by the Small Enterprise Development and Finance Agency (Sedfa), designed to support youth-owned start-up and growing enterprises in South Africa. Targeting entrepreneurs aged between 18 to 35, the programme aims to foster job creation and economic participation through direct financial support. Applicants must be South African citizens, registered with CIPC, with at least 51% youth shareholdership and actively involved in the day to day management of managing their businesses, with its management consisting of 51% youth. The YCF provides a mix of loan and grant funding—up to R2 million for start-ups and R15 million for growth enterprises—through two main channels: Technology & Innovation (Red Channel) and Other Products & Services (Blue Channel).

The fund offers blended finance solutions, with varying loan supported by a grant tailored to the stage and focus of the enterprise. Successful applicants also gain access to essential business development and mentorship support to ensure sustainability. The YCF excludes funding for debt repayment, previously funded activities, illegal enterprises, or applicants with histories of fraud or insolvency. Applications can be submitted online via the Sedfa website, and all submissions undergo a review process, with incomplete applications given a 20-day window for completion. Through this initiative, Sedfa aims to empower the next generation of South African entrepreneurs.

Eligibility Criteria

In line with National Youth Policy, the YCF will be providing support to youth businesses/enterprises who meet the following criteria:

  • Are between the age of 18 to 35 years
  • Registered with CIPC and be prepared to register with SARS & UIF
  • 100% South African owned
  • Are adequately involved in the day-to-day operation and management of the business with at least one or more of the members being a full-time employee of the company, especially the majority shareholder or essential personnel/applicant
  • Prepared to participate in Business Development Support and mentorship (pre and post)
  • Commercially viable, sustainable and feasible business idea.

Exclusions

The following transactions will NOT be considered:

  • Funding towards a debt owed by the applicant with another lending institution (existing debt)
  • Activities that have already been funded by other government departments or parastatals (double-dipping)
  • Government and SOE officials are excluded
  • Fall within the gambling, pyramid sales scheme, loan shark and operates illegal activities etc., as guided by credit policy and grant policies
  • Have a record of fraud and corruption
  • Where the owner/applicant is an un-rehabilitated insolvent
